# The problem

I consider myself extremely lucky to be living through times of extreme change. Of course, it depends on which lens you look through before deciding if extreme change is a good thing or a bad thing.

For example, software engineers and STEM researchers/entrepreneurs might argue that things are going _extremely_ well. The pace of progress has never been faster and great breakthroughs are just around the corner (artificial intelligence, quantum computing, longevity, etc.).

Conversely, we are also seeing catastrophicly negative changes all around us in the natural world. The same systems that have given rise to incredible technological advancements have also been destroying the ecosystems around us and have caused untold conscious suffering. Not to mention making our planet less habitable and abundant for humans as well.

How can we transform our political, economical and academic systems to ensure we're operating in a positive-sum environment? I think we can.

# The hypothesis
_We can live in a high-tech, naturally abundant world where humans and all other living creatures can thrive together._

# The solution
This is what I will explore.
